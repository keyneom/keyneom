    Humility

    noun

    • The term “humility” comes from the Latin word humilitas, a noun related to the adjective humilis, which may be translated as “humble”, but also as “grounded”
    • “Humility is not thinking less of yourself, it’s thinking of yourself less.” ― CS Lewis
    • the quality or state of not thinking you are better than other people
    • modesty, lacking pretence, not believing that you are superior to others . . . shun[ing] public adulation, and . . . never boastful
